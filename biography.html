Selena Gomez is an American singer, actress, and producer born on July 22, 1992, in Grand Prairie, Texas. She began her career in the entertainment industry at a young age, starring in the children's television series Barney & Friends. However, she gained widespread recognition for her role as Alex Russo in Disney Channel's hit show Wizards of Waverly Place, which aired from 2007 to 2012.

In addition to her acting career, Gomez is a successful musician. She formed the band Selena Gomez & the Scene and released several albums, including Kiss & Tell and A Year Without Rain. Her solo career has also flourished with hit singles like "Come & Get It," "Good for You," and "Lose You to Love Me."

Beyond her artistic endeavors, Selena is known for her philanthropic efforts. She has been an advocate for mental health awareness and has supported various charitable causes through her work with organizations like UNICEF.

Selena continues to evolve as an artist and a public figure, using her platform to inspire and empower others. Her resilience and authenticity have made her a beloved figure in popular culture.
